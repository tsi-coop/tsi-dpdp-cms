The Compliance Enforcement Service (CES)

Technically enforcing data deletion is a significant compliance challenge. We handle this via a specialized, periodic background service that bridges the gap between Legal Mandates and Technical Deletion.

1 Nightly Engine

The CES runs periodically (once every 24 hours). It scans every principal's consent state and cross-references them against active retention policies to identify required purge actions.

The Enforcement Lifecycle

Scenario: Consent Withdrawal

A1

Immediate Deactivation: Processing stops instantly to prevent unauthorized use.

A2

Scheduled Purge: Background scrub is scheduled according to the 7-year or 2-year retention policy.

Scenario: Erasure Request

B1

Priority Queue: The request is logged as a priority compliance task.

B2

Next-Run Purge: The purge is initiated immediately in the subsequent CES run, overriding standard retention.

Section 6 & 11 Compliant Evidence

The TSI DPDP CMS ensures that every interaction is captured as a "Compliance Artifact". Auditors can trace the complete data lifecycle of a principal through these four definitive touchpoints:

01 Notice

Collection Proof

Verification of policy version, timestamp, and IP address at the moment of consent capture.

02 Processing

Access Logs

Immutable log of every Processor API call validated by the CMS against the active consent state.

03 Governance

Rights History

Full trail of grievance redressal, corrections, and DPO-initiated status transitions.

04 Erasure

Purge Evidence

Cryptographic proof of hard-purge completion, ensuring PII is permanently removed or masked.

Audit logs are stored in a dedicated schema (`audit_logs`) and are logically separated from operational processing data to maintain high-assurance integrity.
